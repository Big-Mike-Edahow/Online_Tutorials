Constructed in the early 1900's in Medicine Bow, Wyoming, the Virginian Hotel was a meeting place for business men to do their wheelin' an' dealin', as well as a place for cowboys and railroad workers to stay.


In this trucking adventure, Big Mike is having "The Virginian Hamburger" for lunch before heading west to the Great State of Utah, where he'll descend down Parley's Canyon into the Salt Lake Valley.


Plummer, Idaho is named after the outlaw Henry Plummer, who had a hideout near the current townsite. Plummer was elected sheriff of Bannock, Montana in 1863. However, in 1864 Plummer was hanged for being the leader of a band of outlaws called the Road Agents.


In this vacation adventure, Big Mike is having a "Carolina Burger" with crinkle cut fries. After refueling with food and fuel, his next stop is St. Maries, Idaho. There he'll visit historical sites and take pictures.
